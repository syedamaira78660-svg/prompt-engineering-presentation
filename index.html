A Prompt Engineering Companion

Most people order AI
like they're mumbling at a waiter.

Imagine walking into a dhaba or a high-end restaurant in Lahore or Karachi, sitting down, and telling the waiter:

"Bhai, kuch khanay ko le ao." Brother, bring me something to eat.

What do you get? Maybe a plate of cold daal. Maybe a fiery mutton karahi. Maybe just a dry naan. Technically, it's food — but is it what you actually wanted? Probably not.

This is exactly how most of us interact with AI. We drop a vague, two-word request into ChatGPT, get a generic, textbook-style wall of text back, and complain that "AI is overhyped, it's no fun." But the problem isn't the AI. It's the order.

That's where Prompt Engineering comes in. Don't let the heavy, technical name fool you — it isn't computer coding. It's simply the art of knowing exactly how to talk to a machine to get the perfect result on your first try.
